France - Alsace

5 hours from London - Change at Lille

The background image is of a town riverside in Colmar - Alsace

La Grande Est, a picturesque region in northeastern France, offers a delightful blend of natural beauty, rich history, and cultural charm. As you travel through its verdant landscapes, you'll encounter rolling vineyards of the Champagne region, the misty Vosges mountains, and the Rhine River winding along the German border. The region's history comes alive in Strasbourg, home to the majestic Gothic Cathédrale Notre-Dame and the charming half-timbered houses of La Petite France. In Reims, the Cathedral of Notre-Dame stands as a beacon of French coronation history, while the Alsace Wine Route takes you through idyllic villages like Riquewihr and Eguisheim, famed for their colorful facades and cobblestone streets. Whether savoring a glass of champagne or marveling at medieval fortresses, La Grande Est is a feast for the senses.

Germany

3 hours from Paris - Gare de l'Est

The background image is of a large white castle with the Bavarian Alps behind it

The Bavarian Alps and Munich offer a captivating journey through breathtaking landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. As you approach the Alps, you'll be awed by snow-capped peaks, crystal-clear lakes like Königssee, and storybook castles such as Neuschwanstein, which inspired Disney's fairy-tale designs. Munich, Bavaria’s lively capital, combines modern sophistication with historic charm—don’t miss the iconic Marienplatz with its ornate Glockenspiel or the vast Englischer Garten. History buffs can explore the imposing Nymphenburg Palace and the poignant Dachau Memorial Site. Beyond the city, charming towns like Garmisch-Partenkirchen offer access to alpine trails, while beer lovers can indulge in Bavaria's famed brews at traditional beer halls. This region is a treasure trove for nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and culture seekers alike.

Italy

6.5 hours from Paris - Gare de Lyon **Fast route may be blocked - Scenic via Switzerland might be better - or the Bernina Express for a treat

The background image is of the Turin skyline

Northwest Italy is a treasure trove of diverse landscapes, cultural gems, and indulgent experiences. Begin in the elegant city of Turin, the capital of Piedmont, known for its Baroque architecture, rich chocolate, and the iconic Mole Antonelliana. Explore the Langhe region, where rolling hills are blanketed with vineyards producing world-class Barolo and Barbaresco wines. Mantua, a Renaissance jewel in Lombardy, enchants with its grand Palazzo Ducale and lakeside charm. For relaxation, head to the thermal spa town of Acqui Terme, where healing waters have been enjoyed since Roman times. Don’t miss the enchanting Italian Riviera, with colorful coastal villages like Portofino and the chic city of Genoa, steeped in maritime history. Northwest Italy is a perfect blend of relaxation, culture, and breathtaking scenery.

Travel the long way to Italy

Traveling to Turin or Milan from London is a scenic journey that can take several routes. You can start with the Eurostar to Paris, then switch to a high-speed TGV to either city, with Milan reached in just over 7 hours 30 minutes and Turin in 6 hours 30 minutes. Alternatively, for a longer, more picturesque option, you can take the Eurostar to Paris, connect with a TGV to Zurich, and then change onto a scenic train through the Swiss Alps, passing through the tiny, picturesque country of Liechtenstein before arriving in Milan or Turin. This route offers stunning views of the Alps and the opportunity to explore charming Swiss towns along the way.
